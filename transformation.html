🔬 Evidence-Based Training Principles (Read First)

These rules matter more than which exercises you pick. They're drawn from 2022-2024 meta-analyses on hypertrophy and fat-loss training.

PrincipleTargetWhy (evidence)
Weekly volume10-20 hard sets per muscle/weekGraded dose-response up to ~20 sets; diminishing returns after. (Schoenfeld; Jukic 2024)
FrequencyEach muscle 2x/weekTwice-weekly ranks highest for hypertrophy vs once. (BJSM network meta-analysis 2023)
Intensity (effort)Stop 1-2 reps short of failure (RIR 1-2); last set of isolations to failureProximity to failure drives growth. Going to failure every set adds fatigue without much extra gain.
Load6-12 reps for most work; 8 RM compoundsAll loads build muscle near failure; moderate loads balance stimulus + joint stress.
Rest periods2-3 min compounds, 60-90s isolationLonger rest on big lifts preserves volume/strength per set.
Tempo~2s down, controlled up. Avoid >10s repsVery slow reps reduce total load without extra benefit. (Umbrella review 2022)
ProgressionDouble progression: hit top of rep range → add 2.5kgProgressive overload is THE driver of growth. Log every set.
DeloadEvery 6-8 weeks: cut volume ~50% for 1 weekManages fatigue, prevents stalls/injury, especially in a deficit.
Protein (in deficit)~2.0-2.2 g/kg bodyweight (≈180-200g for you)Higher protein in a deficit preserves AND builds muscle. Longland 2016: +1.2kg lean, -4.8kg fat in 4 wks at 2.4g/kg + RT.

Body recomposition is realistic for you. At ~26% body fat and relatively untrained, you can lose fat AND gain muscle simultaneously — the research strongly supports this for your exact profile. This is why 4 months can produce a dramatic visual change.

🏃 Cardio Plan

Cardio is for heart health and calorie deficit — NOT for building muscle. Don't overdo it.

Phase-by-Phase Cardio

PhaseDaily StepsStructured CardioWeekly Calorie Burn

Phase 2 12,000 2x HIIT (15 min: 30s sprint / 60s walk) + 1x LISS (45 min) ~2000 kcal
Phase 3 15,000 3x HIIT (20 min) + 1x LISS (45 min) ~2500 kcal

Cardio Rules

  • Never do HIIT before leg day — you'll compromise your lifts.
  • LISS can be done any day, including rest days.
  • Post-workout cardio: 10 min incline walk after every weight session.
  • Steps are non-negotiable — they matter more than structured cardio for fat loss.
  • Walk after meals (10 min) — improves digestion + adds steps.

Face Fat Reduction — Specific Strategies

  • Sodium control: Max 2000mg/day. Avoid packaged foods, papads, pickles, excessive salt in cooking.
  • Water: 3-4L daily. Flushes sodium, reduces water retention in face.
  • Alcohol: Eliminate completely for the 4 months. Alcohol causes facial bloating, empty calories, and blunts muscle protein synthesis.
  • Sugar: Zero added sugar. Sugar causes inflammation and water retention.
  • Sleep: 7-8 hours. Poor sleep = cortisol = facial puffiness.
  • Overall fat loss: Face fat is the last to go. Be patient. It will lean out as body fat drops below 20%.
